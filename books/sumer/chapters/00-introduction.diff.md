# Line-edit diff — 00-introduction

Each entry shows the original sentence (or fragment) followed by the edited version. Trivial fixes (e.g., punctuation, comma after introductory phrase) are omitted; substantive prose changes are listed below.

## Opening paragraph

- **Original:** "Somewhere around 2100 BCE, in the southern Mesopotamian city of Nippur..."
- **Edited:** "Around 2100 BCE, in the southern Mesopotamian city of Nippur..."
  *Reason: "Somewhere around" is throat-clearing; "around" alone carries the same hedge.*

- **Original:** "It survived the fall of the Third Dynasty of Ur, the rise and collapse of Babylon..."
- **Edited:** "It outlasted the fall of the Third Dynasty of Ur, the rise and collapse of Babylon..."
  *Reason: avoid back-to-back "It survived" / "It survived" repetition (the next sentence begins "It survived because nobody knew it was there"); "outlasted" sharpens the verb without changing meaning.*

## Paragraph 2 (corpus overview)

- **Edited:** "most of them catalogued in the Electronic Text Corpus..."

- **Original:** "and they inhabited a network of city-states spread across the alluvial plain between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers:"

## Paragraph 3 (geographic spread)

- **Original:** "The geographic spread of the finds is worth noting."
- **Edited:** "The geographic spread of the finds matters."
  *Reason: kill the "is worth noting" zombie phrase.*

- **Original:** "excavated by the University of Pennsylvania beginning in the 1890s, and it is Nippur's _edubba_ tradition that dominates the corpus."
- **Edited:** "which the University of Pennsylvania began excavating in the 1890s, and Nippur's _edubba_ tradition dominates the corpus."
  *Reason: removes the "it is X that" cleft (Pinker's classic-style anti-pattern); active verb.*

- **Original:** "But significant literary tablets have also been recovered from Ur..., from Uruk, from the northern Babylonian sites of Sippar and Kish, and from Tell Haddad..."
- **Edited:** "But significant literary tablets have also turned up at Ur..., at Uruk, at the northern Babylonian sites of Sippar and Kish, and at Tell Haddad..."
  *Reason: parallel structure ("at X, at Y, at Z"); "turned up" matches the conversational register.*

- **Original:** "where important Gilgamesh fragments turned up as recently as the 1980s."
- **Edited:** "where important Gilgamesh fragments emerged as recently as the 1980s."
  *Reason: "turned up" was just used in the same sentence; vary the verb.*

- **Original:** "a literary tradition that is simultaneously local..."
- **Edited:** "a literary tradition that is at once local..."
  *Reason: "at once" is shorter and avoids a heavy four-syllable adverb.*

- **Original:** "A composition that was popular in the _edubba_ was copied by many students..."

- **Original:** "are embedded in the corpus itself."
