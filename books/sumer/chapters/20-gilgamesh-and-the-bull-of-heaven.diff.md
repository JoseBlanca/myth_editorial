## Paragraph 8 (challenge to divine authority)

**Original:**

**Edited:**
> Slaying the Bull of Heaven is a direct challenge to divine authority.footnote:[…] A major deity — Inanna, patron of Uruk itself — sent the beast, and mortal heroes destroyed it. The episode dramatises, with considerable force, the limits of divine power.

*Rationale:* "The slaying of … constitutes" is a textbook zombie-noun construction. Pinker's fix: use the verb. "Slaying the Bull of Heaven is …" Same for the second sentence: "its destruction by mortal heroes dramatises" passivises twice. Split into two active sentences: deity sent, heroes destroyed; the episode dramatises. Subject-verb distance drops sharply.
