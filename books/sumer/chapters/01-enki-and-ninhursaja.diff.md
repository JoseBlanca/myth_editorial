## Para 1 (manuscript witnesses)

**Original:**
> It survives in multiple Old Babylonian manuscript witnesses (ca. 2000–1600 BCE) on clay tablets from both Nippur (Sumerian: Nibru) — the religious capital of Sumer — and Ur, the great port city of the south.

**Edited:**
> It survives in several Old Babylonian manuscript witnesses (ca. 2000–1600 BCE) on clay tablets from Nippur (Sumerian: Nibru) — the religious capital of Sumer — and from Ur, the great port city of the south.

*Reason: "multiple" → "several" (less officialese); "from both X and Y" → "from X and from Y" tightens parallel structure.*

---

**Original:**
> The Nippur and Ur recensions represent co-equal witnesses, but they differ in significant ways: the Ur manuscripts include an insertion of international tributes to Dilmun not found in the Nippur copies, while the Nippur tablets provide the base text that the ETCSL composite follows.

**Edited:**
> The Nippur and Ur recensions are co-equal witnesses, but they differ in significant ways: the Ur manuscripts insert international tributes to Dilmun that the Nippur copies lack, while the Nippur tablets supply the base text that the ETCSL composite follows.

*Reason: kill the nominalization "include an insertion of" → active verb "insert"; "represent" → "are"; "not found in" → "lack"; "provide" → "supply" for verb variety.*

## Para 2 (composite text)

**Original:**
> The text as scholars read it is a composite reconstruction assembled from these overlapping fragments, with gaps remaining in the Dilmun description and in several dialogue sections.

**Edited:**
> The text as scholars now read it is a composite, assembled from these overlapping fragments, with gaps remaining in the Dilmun description and in several dialogue sections.

*Reason: drop the redundant "reconstruction" (a composite assembled from fragments is by definition a reconstruction); "now" sharpens the sense of present-tense scholarly reading.*

---

**Original:**
> ...what appears on the page as a continuous narrative is in fact a jigsaw puzzle pieced together by modern editors from school exercises and archive copies that no student or scribe ever read in the form we now have.

**Edited:**
> ...what appears on the page as a continuous narrative is in fact a jigsaw pieced together by modern editors from school exercises and archive copies that no scribe ever read in the form we now have.

*Reason: "jigsaw puzzle" → "jigsaw" (less redundant); "no student or scribe" → "no scribe" (a student was a scribe-in-training; the broader category covers both).*

Note: "no student or scribe" is functionally equivalent to "no scribe" given the edubba context already established; no factual change.

---

**Original:**
> Multiple Old Babylonian copies from both Nippur and Ur scribal schools attest wide circulation of the composition in the _edubba_ curriculum...

**Edited:**
> Multiple Old Babylonian copies from the Nippur and Ur scribal schools attest to wide circulation in the _edubba_ curriculum...

*Reason: "from both X and Y" → "from the X and Y" (parallel structure); "attest wide circulation" → "attest to wide circulation" (idiomatic); drop "of the composition" (already the subject of discussion).*

## Para 3 (scholarship)

**Original:**

**Edited:**
> Both Jacobsen and Kramer analyse the text's paradise motif and botanical imagery at length, making it one of the most discussed Sumerian compositions in modern scholarship.

*Reason: "devote sustained analyses to" is a zombie-noun construction; "analyse … at length" is the verb form.*

*Reason: "follows X as its base" was awkward (since the previous sentence used "follows" already). "Takes" + "offer" gives more verb variety.*

## First incest sequence

**Edited:**

## Cosmic-crisis paragraph

**Original:**
> The situation is desperate beyond what the pantheon can manage. The great gods sit in the dust — a gesture of mourning and helplessness that indicates a crisis of cosmic scale. Enlil — king of the gods, lord of wind and command, supreme authority in Nippur — himself cannot cure Enki.

**Edited:**
> The situation outstrips what the pantheon can manage. The great gods sit in the dust — a gesture of mourning and helplessness that signals a crisis of cosmic scale. Enlil — king of the gods, lord of wind and command, supreme authority in Nippur — cannot cure Enki.

*Reason: "is desperate beyond what" → "outstrips" (one verb instead of three words). "Indicates" → "signals" (a gesture signals; signal is more apt here). Drop "himself" — the appositive already emphasises Enlil's authority; "himself" is redundant emphasis.*
