## Paragraph 1 — opening philological header

- **Orig:** "comprises approximately 260 lines in the composite text"

- *Rationale:* Anglo-Saxon verb for Latinate "comprises approximately"; tighter, same claim.

- **Orig:** "and forms part of the broader Dumuzid dying-god tradition"
- **Edit:** "and belongs to the broader Dumuzid dying-god tradition"
- *Rationale:* "forms part of" is a zombie-noun construction; "belongs to" is a single live verb.

- **Orig:** "Bendt Alster's 1972 monograph provides the critical edition"
- **Edit:** "Bendt Alster's 1972 monograph gives the critical edition"
- *Rationale:* Anglo-Saxon "gives" for Latinate "provides".

- *Rationale:* Kills two zombie-noun constructions ("there is broad manuscript agreement" → live verb "the manuscripts agree broadly"; "as confirmed by" passive → "as Alster's collation confirms" active). Subject-verb distance shortened.

## Paragraph 3 — narrative setup

## Paragraph 12 — Utu transformation

- *Rationale:* "are changed into" → "turn into" (active, Anglo-Saxon, shorter); "enabling" → "letting" (Pinker's preferred Anglo-Saxon equivalent). No factual change.

## Paragraph 21 — closing rescue

- **Orig:** "There is no rescue. There is no last-moment intervention by a sympathetic deity."
