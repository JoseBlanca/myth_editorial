# Line-edit diff: chapter 06 — Enlil and Sud

Method: Pinker (*The Sense of Style*) — kill nominalisations and zombie nouns,
tighten subject-verb distance, prefer parallel structure, smooth paragraph
transitions, remove minor throat-clearing. Footnotes, evidence comments,
comparative-hooks, AsciiDoc structure, and resolved-marker passages
(`_[…]_`, `_(Inference: …)_`) were not touched. American-vs-British spelling
in the body was harmonised toward British, since the prevailing register of
the chapter is already British (`behaviour`, `jewellery`, `modelling`).

Sentence-level changes (non-trivial only) follow.

---

## §1 — Source overview (line 3)

**Original:**
> The composition known to scholars as "Enlil and Sud" is catalogued as ETCSL 1.2.2 and extends to approximately 170 lines, surviving as a well-preserved Old Babylonian composition with no competing recension.

**Edited:**
> The composition known to scholars as "Enlil and Sud" is catalogued as ETCSL 1.2.2, runs to roughly 170 lines, and survives as a well-preserved Old Babylonian composition with no competing recension.

Reasons: parallel verb structure (`is catalogued … runs … survives`); replaced
the participial tail with a finite verb; `extends to approximately` → `runs to
roughly` removes a zombie verb and a four-syllable adverb. No factual change.

## §4 — The blunt approach

**Original:**
> He encounters the young goddess Sud standing in the street and is struck by her beauty; he propositions her directly.

**Edited:**
> He encounters the young goddess Sud standing in the street, is struck by her beauty, and propositions her directly.

Reason: three coordinated finite verbs about one subject; the semicolon split
broke the rhythm. Parallel structure tightens the sentence and matches what is
in fact a single moment.

**Original:**
> The approach is blunt. There is no preamble, no negotiation, no intermediary.

**Edited:**
> The approach is blunt. No preamble, no negotiation, no intermediary.

Reason: dropped the existential `There is`; the bare list hits harder and
matches Pinker's advice on cutting throat-clearing existentials.

**Original:**
> If the reader has just finished chapter 5, the scene carries an unmistakable echo.

**Edited:**
> To a reader who has just finished chapter 5, the scene carries an unmistakable echo.

Reason: rewrote the conditional as a prepositional phrase; the original framed
the reader's experience as hypothetical, but the point is that *for any such
reader*, the echo is there. Same factual content, more direct.

## §5 — Sud's refusal

**Edited:**
> Sud rejects Enlil's initial advance; offended by his bluntness, she rebukes him and tells him she is not a woman to be taken casually.

Reason: collapsed a passive (`is offended`) and a participial (`saying that`)
into tighter active prose; `not a woman who can be taken casually` →
`not a woman to be taken casually` keeps the same meaning with fewer auxiliary
verbs. `his blunt approach` → `his bluntness` (zombie noun → noun).

**Original:**

**Edited:**
> Here the two narratives diverge.

Reason: dropped the leading `And`; the contrast is already in the surrounding
sentences.

## §6 — Nusku as envoy

**Original:**

**Edited:**
> Nusku is Enlil's trusted vizier, the god who carries his messages and executes his commands.

Reason: `on Enlil's behalf` is a four-word periphrasis for `his`; the pronoun
is unambiguous since Enlil is the only candidate antecedent in the sentence.

**Original:**
> His dispatch to Nisaba is not a casual errand.

**Edited:**
> Dispatching him to Nisaba is no casual errand.

Reason: killed the nominalisation `His dispatch` → `Dispatching him`; replaced
the soft `is not a` with the crisper `is no`.

**Original:**
> The shift from direct proposition to mediated courtship is the hinge on which the entire story turns.

**Edited (unchanged):** kept verbatim — the metaphor lands cleanly.

**Original:**
> the mechanism he adopts -- the envoy, the mother, the negotiation -- is precisely the mechanism of proper Sumerian marriage procedure.

**Edited:**
> the mechanism he adopts -- envoy, mother, negotiation -- is precisely the mechanism of proper Sumerian marriage procedure.

Reason: parallel articles dropped from the embedded list for crispness; the
three nouns are already specific from context.
