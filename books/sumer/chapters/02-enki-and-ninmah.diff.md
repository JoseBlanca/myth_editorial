# Line-edit diff: 02-enki-and-ninmah

Each entry shows the original sentence and the edited sentence. Trivial edits (punctuation, whitespace) are omitted. Resolved-marker passages and footnotes are unchanged.

## Opening section

**Original:** All of our witnesses — multiple Old Babylonian copies — were copied in the same city, in the same scribal tradition, during the same period.

**Edited:** Every witness was copied in the same city, in the same scribal tradition, during the same period.

*Rationale:* "All of our witnesses — multiple Old Babylonian copies —" is throat-clearing repetition; the prior sentence already states they are multiple Old Babylonian tablets. Tightened to a cleaner subject.

**Original:** It is a foundational creation narrative widely discussed in every major survey of Sumerian literature.

**Edited:** It is a foundational creation narrative, discussed in every major survey of Sumerian literature.

*Rationale:* "widely discussed in every major survey" is redundant — "every major survey" already implies wide discussion. Removed adverb.

---

**Original:** Kramer, Jacobsen, and every subsequent handbook devote sustained attention to it, and for a straightforward reason: ...

**Edited:** Kramer, Jacobsen, and every subsequent handbook devote sustained attention to it, for a straightforward reason: ...

*Rationale:* Dropped "and" before the colon clause to tighten the connective.

---

**Original:** The text is largely intact but has fragmentary sections in the middle (approximately 6 lines) and at the end (approximately 9 lines).

**Edited:** The text is largely intact but breaks off in the middle (about 6 lines) and at the end (about 9 lines).

*Rationale:* "has fragmentary sections" is a zombie-noun construction; replaced with the verb "breaks off." "Approximately" → "about" twice for plain register.

---

**Original:** The damage is not severe enough to obscure the plot, but it does leave gaps at moments that would interest us...

**Edited:** The damage does not obscure the plot, but it leaves gaps at moments that would interest us...

*Rationale:* Dropped the hedging "is not severe enough to" and emphatic "does" for direct verb form.

---

**Original:** The composition was part of the Old Babylonian scribal curriculum at Nippur, as attested by multiple curricular copies.

**Edited:** The composition belonged to the Old Babylonian scribal curriculum at Nippur, as multiple curricular copies attest.

*Rationale:* Active verb "attest" replaces the passive "as attested by," and "belonged to" is more concrete than "was part of."

---

**Original:** That so many student copies survive tells us something about its importance: ...

**Edited:** So many student copies survive that we can read its importance directly from the evidence: ...

*Rationale:* "That ... tells us something" is a vague meta-comment (zombie-noun construction). Recast to a direct claim about the evidence.

## Pre-creation labour section

**Original:** Before humanity exists, the gods must perform their own physical labour — digging canals, dredging waterways, and piling up silt — to sustain themselves.

**Edited:** Before humanity exists, the gods must do their own physical labour — digging canals, dredging waterways, piling up silt — to sustain themselves.

*Rationale:* "perform their own physical labour" is a nominalization; the participles already describe what they do. "Do" is the natural verb. Dropped the Oxford "and" inside the dash list for rhythm.

---

**Original:** The opening lines lay this out bluntly.

**Edited:** The opening lines say so bluntly.

*Rationale:* "lay this out" is a phrasal verb with vague pronoun reference; "say so" tightens.

---

**Original:** The gods cry out in complaint; their weariness and suffering are presented as the direct motivation for humanity's creation.

**Edited:** The gods cry out in complaint; their weariness and suffering directly motivate humanity's creation.

*Rationale:* "are presented as the direct motivation for" is a classic zombie-noun, passive construction. Replaced with active verb "motivate."

---

**Original:** She rouses her son Enki ... from sleep and urges him to devise a solution to relieve the gods of their toil.

**Edited:** She rouses her son Enki ... from sleep and urges him to devise a way of relieving the gods of their toil.

*Rationale:* "a solution to relieve" → "a way of relieving" eliminates the nominalization "solution"; the gerund flows better.

## Clay and creation section

**Original:** Enki devises the plan: humanity will be created from clay taken from above the _abzu_.

**Edited:** Enki devises the plan: humanity will be made from clay taken from above the _abzu_.

*Rationale:* "created" repeats earlier "creation"; "made" is plainer and avoids elegant variation later when "creation" matters.

---

**Original:** The material is important.

**Edited:** The material matters.

*Rationale:* "is important" → "matters." Verb in place of copula + adjective.

---

**Original:** Clay is what Mesopotamians built with, wrote on, and understood.

**Edited:** Clay was what Mesopotamians built with, wrote on, and understood.

*Rationale:* Tense agreement: the surrounding past-tense "built / wrote / understood" calls for "was."

---

**Original:** Nammu, at Enki's instruction, kneads clay from above the _abzu_ to shape the first human beings, with Ninmah — the great mother goddess — and the birth-goddesses assisting.

**Edited:** At Enki's instruction, Nammu kneads clay from above the _abzu_ to shape the first human beings, with Ninmah — the great mother goddess — and the birth-goddesses assisting.

*Rationale:* Subject-verb distance: moved the prepositional phrase to the front so subject ("Nammu") sits next to its verb ("kneads").

---

**Original:** Here, the process requires a division of labour: Enki provides the concept, Nammu provides the material and the primary shaping, and Ninmah and the birth-goddesses assist in the work.

**Edited:** Here, the work demands a division of labour: Enki provides the concept, Nammu provides the material and the primary shaping, and Ninmah and the birth-goddesses assist.

*Rationale:* "the process requires" → "the work demands": stronger verb, plainer noun. Dropped "in the work" at the end (redundant after the colon list).

---

**Original:** The birth-goddesses assist in the shaping, and the new creatures are assigned the labour that the gods previously performed.

**Edited:** The birth-goddesses help shape the new creatures, who are then assigned the labour the gods previously performed.

*Rationale:* "assist in the shaping" is a zombie noun ("shaping"); replaced with "help shape." Recast as a single subordinated clause for flow; dropped "that" for tighter relative clause.

## Theological-position section

**Original:** The composition establishes a Sumerian answer to the question of why humanity exists: ...

**Edited:** The composition supplies a Sumerian answer to the question of why humanity exists: ...

*Rationale:* "establishes" is bureaucratic; "supplies" is concrete and matches the practical tone of the text.

---

**Original:** Other cultures would develop creation narratives grounded in divine love, cosmic accident, or philosophical necessity.

**Edited:** Other cultures would build creation narratives on divine love, cosmic accident, or philosophical necessity.

*Rationale:* "develop ... grounded in" is verbose; "build ... on" gives one cleaner verb-preposition pair.

---

**Original:** This labour-relief motive is distinct from, though parallel to, the Akkadian _Atrahasis_ creation account, which is a separate textual tradition with its own narrative framework and should not be conflated with the Sumerian version.

**Edited:** This labour-relief motive is distinct from, though parallel to, the Akkadian _Atrahasis_ creation account, a separate textual tradition with its own narrative framework that should not be conflated with the Sumerian version.

*Rationale:* Dropped the relative clause "which is" in favour of an appositive. Tightens the sentence and removes the awkward "and should not."

## Banquet and contest section

**Original:** A banquet follows the creation, at which Enki and Ninmah drink beer and become intoxicated.

**Edited:** A banquet follows the creation, and at it Enki and Ninmah drink beer and become intoxicated.

*Rationale:* The relative "at which" creates an awkward compound sentence; conjoined main clauses ("and at it ...") read more naturally.

---

**Original:** Ninmah challenges Enki: she will create beings with various impairments, and Enki must find a social role for each of them.

**Edited:** Ninmah challenges Enki: she will create beings with various impairments, and Enki must find a social role for each.

*Rationale:* Dropped "of them" — the antecedent is unambiguous, and the shorter form is cleaner.

---

**Original:** The social-integration theme is central: each impaired human is assigned a productive function — the person who cannot bend their hands is made a servant of the king; the blind person becomes a musician; the barren woman is placed in the queen's household.

**Edited:** The social-integration theme is central. Each impaired human receives a productive function: the person who cannot bend their hands is made a servant of the king; the blind person becomes a musician; the barren woman is placed in the queen's household.

*Rationale:* Two-step structure with a period plus colon reads more clearly than dash-after-colon. "Is assigned" → "receives" is more active.

---

**Original:** The blind person is not defined by what they cannot do — see — but by what they can do — make music.

**Edited:** The blind person is defined not by what they cannot do — see — but by what they can do — make music.

*Rationale:* Moved "not" inside the predicate to attach precisely to the contrast ("not by ... but by ..."). Pinker parallel-structure point.

---

**Original:** The contest structure — create an impaired being, find it a role — establishes a divine origin for human physical diversity and for the assignment of social roles based on capacity.

**Edited:** The structure of the contest — create an impaired being, find it a role — establishes a divine origin for human physical diversity and for the assignment of social roles by capacity.

*Rationale:* "The contest structure" reads as a compound noun; "the structure of the contest" is more natural English. "Based on capacity" → "by capacity" is tighter.

## Reversal and Umul section

**Original:** The text goes through capacity after capacity — eating, drinking, standing, sitting, grasping — and marks each one as absent.

**Edited:** The text runs through capacity after capacity — eating, drinking, standing, sitting, grasping — and marks each one as absent.

*Rationale:* "Runs through" matches the relentless cataloguing pace better than the bland "goes through."

---

**Original:** Umul is impaired in every way. It is a being with no capacity at all — a creature that cannot be placed anywhere in the social order because it cannot do anything.

**Edited:** Umul is impaired in every way, a being with no capacity at all — a creature that cannot be placed anywhere in the social order because it cannot do anything.

*Rationale:* Combined into one sentence with appositive; the period created a needless stop, and the appositive clarifies the relationship.

---

**Original:** Ninmah is unable to assign any social role to Umul; she cannot integrate this creation into the human order, and she admits defeat.

**Edited:** Ninmah cannot assign any social role to Umul; she cannot integrate this creation into the human order, and she admits defeat.

*Rationale:* "is unable to" → "cannot." Plain verb in place of zombie construction; also creates parallelism with the next clause's "cannot integrate."

---

**Original:** ... the exact resolution is debated among translators due to damage in the final lines.

**Edited:** ... the exact resolution is debated among translators because of damage in the final lines.

*Rationale:* "Due to" used adverbially is awkward; "because of" is the cleaner causal preposition.

---

**Original:** What survives suggests that the composition ends with an acknowledgment of Enki's cleverness ...

**Edited:** What survives suggests that the composition ends by acknowledging Enki's cleverness ...

*Rationale:* "ends with an acknowledgment of" is a zombie-noun ("acknowledgment"). Replaced with the gerund "by acknowledging."
