# Line-edit diff: 12-inanna-and-shu-kale-tuda

Each entry shows the original sentence (or clause) and the edited version. Footnotes, resolved-marker passages, EVIDENCE/COMPARATIVE-HOOK comments, and AsciiDoc structure are unchanged.

## Header paragraph (intro to the composition)

- **Original:** "The text contains significant fragmentary sections, particularly in the descriptions of the three plagues that form the narrative's central action."
- **Edited:** "The text breaks badly in places, especially in the descriptions of the three plagues that drive the central action."
- *Reason:* Kills the nominalization "contains significant fragmentary sections"; replaces "form...central action" (zombie noun) with active "drive the central action."

- **Original:** "but significant gaps remain in the plague description sections across all witnesses, classifying the text as single-prevalent-reconstructed."
- **Edited:** "but every witness leaves the plague descriptions full of gaps, classifying the text as single-prevalent-reconstructed."
- *Reason:* Active subject ("every witness leaves") rather than abstract ("gaps remain ... across all witnesses"); shorter subject-verb distance.

## Second intro paragraph

- **Original:** "The composition combines sexual violence, divine pursuit, and cosmic justice in a distinctive narrative structure: crime, three escalating plagues, judgment."
- **Edited:** "The composition fuses sexual violence, divine pursuit, and cosmic justice into a distinctive narrative shape: crime, three escalating plagues, judgment."
- *Reason:* "fuses ... into a shape" is more concrete than "combines ... in a structure" (less abstract noun).

- **Original:** "The previous chapter presented Inanna as the irresistible destroyer ... This chapter presents something different"
- **Edited:** "The previous chapter showed Inanna as the irresistible destroyer ... This chapter shows something different"
- *Reason:* "showed/shows" is plainer and more concrete than the slightly bureaucratic "presented/presents."

## Failing-garden paragraph

- **Original:** "The opening, though damaged, establishes a portrait of futility."
- **Edited:** "The opening, though damaged, sketches a portrait of futility."
- *Reason:* "Sketches" is more vivid than the bureaucratic "establishes."

- **Original:** "where the canal and the furrow were the difference between survival and starvation"
- **Edited:** "where the canal and the furrow stood between survival and starvation"
- *Reason:* Concrete verb ("stood between") replaces flat copula ("were the difference between"); shorter, more visual.

## Shade-tree paragraph

- **Original:** "The mortal who could not make anything live has, by consulting the stars, discovered how to cultivate successfully."
- **Edited:** "The mortal who could not make anything live has, by reading the stars, discovered how to cultivate."
- *Reason:* "Reading the stars" is the natural English idiom and more concrete than "consulting"; the adverb "successfully" is redundant after "discovered how to cultivate."

## After the rape

- **Edited:** "What matters to the narrative is not the psychology of the act but its consequences, and those consequences will consume the rest of the composition."

- **Original:** "The perpetrator is hidden by the sheer scale of humanity, and Inanna's response to this problem will define the theological logic of the composition."
- **Edited:** "The sheer scale of humanity hides the perpetrator, and how Inanna answers that problem will define the theological logic of the composition."

## First-plague reconstruction paragraph

- *Reason:* "Strikes" is more vivid than the limp "affects"; the present-tense "fills/fouls" matches the present-tense narration in the same clause; "fouls" is sharper than "polluted."

- **Original:** "the corruption of water is an attack on the foundations of existence."
- **Edited:** "to corrupt water is to attack the foundations of existence."
- *Reason:* Kills two zombie nouns ("the corruption of," "an attack on") and turns them into matched verbs.
