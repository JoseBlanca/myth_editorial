# Line-edit diff: 18-marriage-of-martu

Non-trivial changes only (typo/whitespace fixes not listed). Factual content, footnotes, and resolved-marker passages are unchanged.

## Opening paragraph

- **Original**: "its ethnographic content has attracted consistent scholarly interest as a rare Sumerian literary witness to Amorite cultural perception from the urban Sumerian viewpoint."
- **Edited**: "the ethnographic content has drawn steady scholarly interest as a rare Sumerian literary witness to how urban Sumerians perceived Amorite culture."
- **Reason**: Unpacks the zombie-noun chain "Amorite cultural perception from the urban Sumerian viewpoint" into an active clause; swaps "attracted consistent" for the plainer "drawn steady."

## Framing paragraph

- **Original**: "a story in which the central question is not whether..."
- **Edited**: "a story whose central question is not whether..."
- **Reason**: Tightens subject-verb distance with a possessive relative.

## Martu introduction paragraph

- **Original**: "The name requires some unpacking."
- **Edited**: "The name needs some unpacking."
- **Reason**: "Requires" is bureaucratic where "needs" is direct (Pinker, plain-verb preference).

- **Original**: "He is not merely associated with these peoples; he is, in the logic of Sumerian theology, the divine reality of which they are the human expression."
- **Edited**: "He is not merely associated with these peoples; in the logic of Sumerian theology, he is the divine reality of which they are the human expression."
- **Reason**: Moves the parenthetical out from between subject and verb to shorten subject-verb distance.

## Numushda paragraph

- **Original**: "He is not one of the great gods of the Sumerian pantheon — he is a city god, a local patron, the kind of deity whose importance was enormous within his own walls and negligible outside them."
- **Edited**: "He is not one of the great gods of the Sumerian pantheon. He is a city god, a local patron, the kind of deity whose importance loomed enormous within his own walls and negligible outside them."
- **Reason**: Breaks an over-long sentence at the dash, and replaces flat copula "was" with the active verb "loomed" for rhythm.

- **Original**: "it is to this festival that the god Martu comes."
- **Edited**: "to this festival the god Martu comes."
- **Reason**: Removes the "it is ... that" cleft, a classic Pinker target for throat-clearing.

## Courtship paragraph

- **Original**: "Martu sees the daughter of Numushda and falls in love with her, desiring to marry her."
- **Edited**: "Martu sees the daughter of Numushda, falls in love with her, and resolves to marry her."
- **Reason**: Parallel triple of finite verbs (sees / falls / resolves) replaces the trailing participial "desiring," matching the next sentence's tricolon rhythm.

- **Original**: "does not, as a rule, spend many lines on the psychology of attraction."
- **Edited**: "does not, as a rule, linger on the psychology of attraction."
- **Reason**: "Linger" is one verb with a more precise image than the periphrastic "spend many lines on."

## Suitability paragraph

- **Original**: "the question of his suitability — as a nomadic god among settled city-dwellers — becomes..."
- **Edited**: "the question of his suitability — a nomadic god among settled city-dwellers — becomes..."
- **Reason**: Drops redundant "as"; the appositive is cleaner.

- **Original**: "This is where the composition finds its real subject."
- **Edited**: "Here the composition finds its real subject."
- **Reason**: Eliminates the "This is where ... that" cleft.

## Friend's objection paragraph

- **Original**: "The objections are articulated not by the bride herself, and not by her father Numushda, but by a female friend. This friend delivers an extended warning speech describing..."
- **Edited**: "The objections come not from the bride herself, and not from her father Numushda, but from a female friend. The friend delivers an extended warning speech that describes..."
- **Reason**: "Come" is plainer than the nominalization "are articulated"; "this friend" → "the friend" reads less like a pointer variable; adds a relative clause for clarity.

## Catalogue paragraph

- **Original**: "The catalogue of nomadic habits functions as a definition by negation:"
- **Edited**: "The catalogue of nomadic habits defines by negation:"
- **Reason**: Kills the nominalization "functions as a definition."

- **Original**: "Every item in the list is a marker of urbanity that the nomads lack."
- **Edited**: "Every item in the list marks a feature of urbanity that the nomads lack."
- **Reason**: "Is a marker of" → "marks" (zombie-noun fix).

- **Original**: "no investment in place, no participation in the great settled project..."
- **Edited**: "no investment in place, no share in the great settled project..."
- **Reason**: "Share" is one syllable against "participation"'s five, and keeps the parallel noun-chain rhythm.

## Summary-catalogue paragraph

- **Original**: "The friend's speech functions as an ethnographic catalogue that reflects the urban Sumerian perception of Amorite peoples: the nomads are defined by what they lack relative to settled civilisation, including proper food preparation, permanent dwelling, and funerary rites."
- **Edited**: "The friend's speech works as an ethnographic catalogue that reflects how urban Sumerians perceived the Amorite peoples: the nomads are defined by what they lack relative to settled civilisation — proper food preparation, permanent dwelling, and funerary rites."
- **Reason**: "Functions" → "works"; unpacks the noun cluster "urban Sumerian perception of Amorite peoples" into an active clause; replaces "including" with a dash, which reads more decisively.

## Bride's acceptance paragraph

- **Original**: "choosing the nomadic god over the objections raised by her friend."
- **Edited**: "choosing the nomadic god over the objections her friend has raised."
- **Reason**: Replaces reduced passive with active voice.

- **Original**: "The bride's acceptance constitutes the resolution of the narrative: the cultural boundary between settled and nomadic life is crossed by an act of voluntary choice, not coercion."
- **Edited**: "Her acceptance resolves the narrative: the cultural boundary between settled and nomadic life is crossed by a voluntary choice, not by coercion."
- **Reason**: "Constitutes the resolution of" → "resolves" (nominalization killed); "an act of voluntary choice" → "a voluntary choice" (drop the zombie "act of"); parallel prepositions "by ... by" in the contrast.

## Comedy paragraph

- **Original**: "The composition functions as a literary comedy of cultural encounter, staging the tension between settled Sumerian civilisation and the nomadic Amorite world in the form of a marriage negotiation."
- **Edited**: "The composition works as a literary comedy of cultural encounter, staging the tension between settled Sumerian civilisation and the nomadic Amorite world through a marriage negotiation."
- **Reason**: "Functions as" → "works as"; "in the form of" → "through" (shorter, concrete preposition).

- **Original**: "The word 'comedy' here is used in its structural sense:"
- **Edited**: "The word 'comedy' here carries its structural sense:"
- **Reason**: Replaces bland passive "is used" with active "carries."

## Didactic-function paragraph

- **Original**: "The didactic function of the text lies in the bride's willingness to accept integration across cultural boundaries, a message that may reflect..."
- **Edited**: "The text teaches through the bride's willingness to accept integration across cultural boundaries — a message that may reflect..."
- **Reason**: "The didactic function of the text lies in" is five zombie-noun words for one verb, "teaches"; dash replaces comma for cleaner apposition.

## Irony paragraph

- **Original**: "That historical context gives the composition a layer of irony that the original audience would have appreciated."
- **Edited**: "That historical context gives the composition a layer of irony the original audience would have appreciated."
- **Reason**: Drops the optional "that" to tighten.

- **Original**: "The horror at nomadic customs was, by the time of the text's transmission, a literary convention rather than a lived anxiety..."
- **Edited**: "The horror at nomadic customs had become, by the time of the text's transmission, a literary convention rather than a lived anxiety..."
- **Reason**: "Had become" captures the shift the paragraph is describing where "was" merely states.

## Closing paragraph

- **Original**: "The civilised-versus-nomadic bride narrative appears across the ancient Near East as a recurring literary form."
- **Edited**: "The civilised-versus-nomadic bride narrative recurs across the ancient Near East as a literary form."
- **Reason**: "Appears ... as a recurring" is redundant; "recurs" folds the idea into the verb.

- **Original**: "allows the tension between these two positions to speak for itself."
- **Edited**: "lets the tension between these two positions speak for itself."
- **Reason**: "Lets ... speak" is idiomatic; "allows ... to speak" is bureaucratic.
