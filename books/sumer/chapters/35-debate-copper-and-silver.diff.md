# Line-edit diff: 35-debate-copper-and-silver

Each entry shows the original sentence (or clause) on the left of the arrow and the edited version on the right. Factual content, footnotes, and resolved-marker passages are unchanged.

## Paragraph 1 (opening framing)

- "all from Nippur, compared with sixty or seventy for Hoe and Plough or Ewe and Grain"
  → "all from Nippur, against sixty or seventy for Hoe and Plough or Ewe and Grain"
  (Tighter preposition; "compared with" is throat-clearing where "against" carries the contrast directly.)

- "This suggests more limited circulation in the _edubba_, the scribal school where these texts were copied and taught."
  → "The text seems to have circulated less widely in the _edubba_, the scribal school where these compositions were copied and taught."
  (Killed zombie noun "more limited circulation" → verb "circulated less widely"; replaced the demonstrative "This suggests" with a concrete subject. "Texts" → "compositions" to avoid repeating "text" in the next paragraph.)

  (Broke a long sentence into two; folded the Enlil gloss into the first-mention appositive rather than a separate copular sentence, which tightens subject-verb distance and removes the clumsy "Enlil. Enlil is" repetition.)

## Section "Silver's claim"

- "Silver claims to occupy a higher station in the order of civilisation because it is associated with occasions of dignity rather than occasions of labour."
  → "Silver occupies a higher station in the order of civilisation because it belongs to occasions of dignity rather than occasions of labour."
  (Dropped the hedging frame "claims to" — the sentence is already inside a paragraph summarising Silver's argument, so the hedge is redundant. "Is associated with" → "belongs to": kills the passive/nominal zombie.)

- "the metal used for adornment and royal treasure holds a higher place than the metal that gets dirty in the fields"
  → "the metal used for adornment and royal treasure stands above the metal that gets dirty in the fields"
  (Plainer verb with a concrete spatial image; "holds a higher place than" is a nominalised paraphrase of "stands above".)

- "In any society that distinguishes between work and ceremony"
  → "In any society that distinguishes work from ceremony"
  (Standard idiom "distinguish X from Y" is tighter than "distinguish between X and Y".)

## Section "Copper's reply"

- "Its case is built not on prestige but on indispensability"
  → "Its case rests not on prestige but on indispensability"
  (Active verb "rests" replaces the passive "is built".)

- "Copper supplies the adzes that make ploughs."
  → "Copper supplies the adzes that shape ploughs."
  (Plainer, more specific verb; adzes shape wood, they do not merely "make" a plough in the abstract.)

- "Copper supplies the axes that chop firewood."
  → "Copper supplies the axes that cut firewood."
  (Avoids "chop" appearing three times in the paragraph — already used for hoes chopping weeds and weeds being chopped. "Cut" reads more naturally for axes and firewood.)

- "The insult is precise. Silver boasted of prestige, and Copper replies that Silver's prestige amounts to being buried in dirt between uses."
  → "The insult is precise. Silver boasted of prestige; Copper replies that Silver's prestige amounts to being buried in dirt between uses."
  (Semicolon replaces the comma-plus-"and" to sharpen the rhetorical contrast between Silver's boast and Copper's riposte.)

## Section "The verdict"

- "it is the only one in which the contestant who does not give the opening speech goes on to win the contest"
  → "it is the only one in which the contestant who does not open the debate goes on to win it"
  (Killed the nominal phrase "give the opening speech" → verb "open the debate". "Win the contest" shortened to "win it" — the antecedent is obvious.)

- "Enlil's judgment follows the logic that the composition has been building from the start."
  → "Enlil's judgment follows the logic the composition has been building from the start."
  (Dropped the light "that" — the relative is clear without it and the sentence reads faster.)

- "and for all of these tasks, the civilisation of southern Mesopotamia required copper"
  → "and for all of these tasks the civilisation of southern Mesopotamia required copper"
  (Dropped the comma after "tasks" — the fronted prepositional phrase runs cleanly into the main clause without a pause that would interrupt the parallel rhythm of the preceding list.)

## Section "The composition in context"

  (Active voice with a concrete subject; the passive hides the agent.)

- "built from the limited fragmentary witnesses recovered from Nippur, supplemented by earlier scholarly work..."

  (Parallel structure: two co-ordinate participial phrases joined by "and" rather than stacked with a comma, which avoids a comma-splice feel.)

- "where every field depended on a dug canal and every harvest depended on a sharpened sickle"
  → "where every field depended on a dug canal and every harvest on a sharpened sickle"
  (Elided the repeated verb "depended" in the second conjunct — a standard parallel-list tightening.)

- "where gold, silver, bronze, and iron represent declining cosmic epochs"
  → "where gold, silver, bronze, and iron stand for declining cosmic epochs"
  (Plainer verb "stand for" in place of the Latinate "represent", matching the book's narrative register.)
