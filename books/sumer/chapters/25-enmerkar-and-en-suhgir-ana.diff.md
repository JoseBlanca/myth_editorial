## Manuscript-witness paragraph

**Before:** The text is well-preserved across Old Babylonian manuscripts ...

**After:** The text survives well across Old Babylonian manuscripts ...

Rationale: Replace the passive copula "is well-preserved" with the active verb "survives well", which also avoids repeating "well-preserved" from the previous paragraph.

**Before:** ... bringing the contest between Uruk and Aratta to a conclusion in Uruk's favour.

**After:** ... closing the contest between Uruk and Aratta in Uruk's favour.

Rationale: Kill the zombie-noun construction "bringing ... to a conclusion" -> the single verb "closing".

## "After the lengthy diplomatic exchanges" paragraph

**Before:** There are no more messengers trudging across seven mountain ranges with speeches compressed into their memories. There are no more verbal contests in which the medium of communication itself becomes the issue. In this composition, the conflict between Uruk and Aratta is decided not by words but by magic -- and not by kings but by proxies.

**After:** No more messengers trudge across seven mountain ranges with speeches compressed into their memories. No more verbal contests turn the medium of communication itself into the issue. Here, magic decides the conflict between Uruk and Aratta -- not words, and not kings but proxies.

Rationale: Two existential "There are no more ..." openings in a row are throat-clearing; replaced with active verbs ("trudge", "turn"). Third sentence: kill the passive "is decided" and the wordy "In this composition" in favour of "Here, magic decides ...".

## Sorcery-challenge paragraph

**Before:** En-suhgir-ana is lord of Aratta, the distant wealthy city that has been the rival of Uruk throughout the Aratta cycle ...

**After:** En-suhgir-ana is lord of Aratta, the distant wealthy city that has rivalled Uruk throughout the cycle ...

Rationale: Kill the nominalisation ("has been the rival of" -> "has rivalled") and drop the redundant "Aratta" since the cycle was just named.

**Before:** The contest in this composition shifts from the diplomatic and rhetorical exchanges of the preceding Enmerkar poem to a direct sorcery challenge: the conflict is decided through magical power rather than rhetoric.

**After:** The contest in this composition shifts from the diplomatic and rhetorical exchanges of the preceding Enmerkar poem to a direct sorcery challenge: magical power, not rhetoric, will decide the conflict.

Rationale: Flip the passive "is decided through ... rather than rhetoric" to the active "magical power, not rhetoric, will decide the conflict". Tighter and more concrete.

**Before:** Diplomacy has been tried and has failed to settle the question permanently.

**After:** Diplomacy has been tried and has failed to settle the question.

Rationale: Drop "permanently" -- redundant given "settle".

**Before:** Writing itself has been invented in the course of the argument, and still the lord of Aratta has not conceded final defeat.

**After:** Writing itself has been invented in the course of the argument, and still the lord of Aratta has not conceded.

Rationale: Drop "final defeat" -- "conceded" already implies it; tightens the sentence rhythm.

## "En-suhgir-ana boasts" paragraph

**Before:** The choice of champion is significant.

**After:** The choice of champion matters.

Rationale: Kill the nominalisation "is significant" -> the punchier verb "matters". Common Pinker substitution.

**Before:** ... and he has acquired a practitioner of formidable reputation.

**After:** ... and he has secured a practitioner of formidable reputation.

Rationale: "Acquired" treats the sorcerer as inventory; "secured" preserves the meaning while better matching the diplomatic-political register of the sentence.

## "Urgirinuna, a male sorcerer" paragraph

**Before:** ... a specialist whose skills are available to whoever can afford them.

**After:** ... a specialist whose skills go to whoever can afford them.

Rationale: Kill the copula+adjective ("are available") in favour of the active verb ("go to").

**Before:** He is, in the logic of the narrative, a free agent of considerable power and no particular loyalty, and this combination makes him dangerous.

**After:** In the logic of the narrative, he is a free agent of considerable power and no particular loyalty, and the combination makes him dangerous.

Rationale: Front the adverbial "In the logic of the narrative" so the subject ("he") sits next to its verb ("is"); shorter subject-verb distance. Also "this combination" -> "the combination": the antecedent is unambiguous.

## "Urgirinuna travels to Eresh" paragraph

**Before:** Urgirinuna travels to Eresh -- the city of the goddess Nisaba -- not directly to Uruk, ...

**After:** Urgirinuna travels to Eresh -- the city of the goddess Nisaba -- rather than directly to Uruk, ...

Rationale: "Not directly to Uruk" reads as a fragment-style negation; "rather than directly to Uruk" makes the contrast explicit and parallel.

**Before:** The target selection is methodical.

**After:** He chooses his target methodically.

Rationale: Classic Pinker move -- nominalisation ("target selection is methodical") replaced with subject + verb + adverb ("He chooses his target methodically"). Concrete agent, active verb.

**Before:** It is not destruction but perversion, the subversion of natural order by supernatural means.

**After:** It is not destruction but perversion, subverting the natural order by supernatural means.

Rationale: Kill the zombie noun "the subversion of" in favour of the participle "subverting"; adds "the" before "natural order" to fix the now-ungrammatical bare noun.

## "The sorcerer's magic threatens" paragraph

**Before:** ... the redirection of milk is an attack on the entire economic infrastructure.

**After:** ... redirecting milk attacks the entire economic infrastructure.

Rationale: Two zombie nouns in one short clause ("the redirection of", "is an attack on") collapsed into a single subject-verb pair ("redirecting milk attacks"). Standard Pinker compression.

**After:** Her title -- "Wise Woman" -- carries formal authority, not merely a description of her character.

## "The duels deserve attention" paragraph

**Before:** The sequence operates on a logic that is not immediately obvious but becomes clear on examination.

**After:** The sequence runs on a logic that is not immediately obvious but becomes clear on examination.

Rationale: "Operates" is abstract corporate-speak; "runs" is concrete and sharper.

**Before:** In each case, the creature that ought to be the prey proves to be the predator.

**After:** In each case, the creature that ought to be prey proves to be predator.

Rationale: Drop the two definite articles -- the categorical sense ("prey", "predator" as roles) reads cleaner without them and preserves the parallelism.
