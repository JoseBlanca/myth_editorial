# Line-edit diff: 28-eridu-flood-story

Each entry shows the original sentence followed by the edited sentence. Trivial whitespace-only changes are omitted. No factual content, footnotes, or resolved-marker passages were altered.

## Paragraph 1 (opening)

- **Original:** "The composition known to scholars as the 'Eridu Genesis' is catalogued as ETCSL 1.7.4 and survives on a single tablet, CBS 10673, recovered from Nippur (Sumerian: Nibru) — the religious capital of Sumer, seat of Enlil's temple the E-kur — the sole known manuscript of the Sumerian flood narrative."
- **Edited:** "The composition known to scholars as the 'Eridu Genesis' is catalogued as ETCSL 1.7.4 and survives on a single tablet, CBS 10673, recovered from Nippur (Sumerian: Nibru) — the religious capital of Sumer, seat of Enlil's temple the E-kur. It is the sole known manuscript of the Sumerian flood narrative."
- **Reason:** Triple em-dash construction strained the reader. Splitting into two sentences shortens subject-verb distance and clarifies what "sole known manuscript" refers to.

- **Original:** "There is no second copy. No overlapping fragment from Ur or Lagash or any other site supplements what this one tablet preserves."
- **Edited:** "There is no second copy, and no overlapping fragment from Ur, Lagash, or any other site supplements what this one tablet preserves."
- **Reason:** Joined two closely related sentences; replaced "or ... or" chain with parallel comma list.

- **Original:** "Every scholarly edition of the Sumerian flood story depends entirely on this one piece of baked clay, and what that piece of clay has lost, scholarship cannot recover."
- **Edited:** "Every scholarly edition of the Sumerian flood story depends entirely on this piece of baked clay, and what the clay has lost, scholarship cannot recover."
- **Reason:** Tightened by removing repeated "one" and "that piece of clay" pointer phrasing.

## Paragraph 2 (tablet condition)

- **Original:** "The tablet is in extremely poor condition, with large portions of every column broken or illegible; the text that survives represents only fragments of a much longer composition."
- **Edited:** "The tablet is in extremely poor condition. Large portions of every column are broken or illegible, and the surviving text represents only fragments of a much longer composition."
- **Reason:** Broke a with-participial run-on into two crisper sentences; replaced "the text that survives" with the more direct "the surviving text."

- **Original:** "Very fragmentary; the creation of man and the boat-building details are largely missing."
- **Edited:** "The creation of man and the details of boat-building are largely missing."
- **Reason:** "Very fragmentary;" was throat-clearing duplicating the preceding sentence. Slight re-order reads more naturally.

- **Reason:** "Drawn" is more active than "received" for attention as the direct object.

- **Original:** "That attention is understandable."
- **Edited:** "The attention is understandable."
- **Reason:** Mild tightening; "the" is less demonstrative, smoother bridge.

- **Original:** "... a narrative pattern that would later appear in Akkadian literature and, through channels that remain debated, in the Hebrew Bible."
- **Edited:** "... a narrative pattern that would later appear in Akkadian literature and, through channels still debated, in the Hebrew Bible."
- **Reason:** Replaced "that remain" with "still" — shorter, same meaning.

- **Original:** "The Eridu Genesis, as it is conventionally called, is one of the most fragmentary major Sumerian compositions; the surviving text amounts to disconnected passages from a work that was originally much longer."
- **Edited:** "The Eridu Genesis, as it is conventionally called, is one of the most fragmentary major Sumerian compositions, and the surviving text amounts to disconnected passages from a work originally much longer."
- **Reason:** Replaced semicolon with comma-conjunction for smoother flow; dropped "that was" (zombie relative clause).

## Paragraph 4 (Akkadian traditions)

- **Reason:** Comma-conjunction reads as a continuation; semicolon felt abrupt.
