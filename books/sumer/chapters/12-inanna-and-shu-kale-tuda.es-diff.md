# Diff — Chapter 12: Inanna and Shu-kale-tuda (English → Peninsular Spanish)

Representative sample of sentence-level renderings plus every non-trivial decision. Glossary-locked terms are marked [G].

## Title

- EN: `Inanna and Shu-kale-tuda: The Gardener's Crime`
- ES: `Inanna y Shu-kale-tuda: el crimen del jardinero`
- Note: Sentence-case subtitle per Spanish convention; «Shu-kale-tuda» transliteration unchanged (no standard Spanish form).

## Opening paragraph

- EN: `The composition known as "Inanna and Shu-kale-tuda" is catalogued as ETCSL 1.3.3 and survives on Old Babylonian tablets from Nippur.`
- ES: `La composición conocida como «Inanna y Shu-kale-tuda» figura en el catálogo ETCSL con la referencia 1.3.3 y se conserva en tablillas paleobabilonias procedentes de Nippur.` [G: Nippur]
- Notes: Guillemets for titles; «Old Babylonian» → «paleobabilonias»; Akkadian/standard form Nippur per glossary (no need to re-gloss — already glossed in earlier chapters).

- EN: `The text breaks badly in places, especially in the descriptions of the three plagues that drive the central action.`
- ES: `El texto presenta roturas graves en varios puntos, sobre todo en las descripciones de las tres plagas que impulsan la acción central.`
- Note: «breaks badly» → «presenta roturas graves»; preserves factual meaning (material damage to the tablet).

- EN: `classifying the text as single-prevalent-reconstructed`
- ES: `lo que clasifica el texto como de tradición única y predominante reconstruida`
- Note: Technical pipeline term rendered consistently with chapter 11 («tradición única y predominante»), extended with «reconstruida» to match the English hyphenated compound.

## Crime, pursuit, and consequence

- EN: `The composition fuses sexual violence, divine pursuit, and cosmic justice into a distinctive narrative shape: crime, three escalating plagues, judgment.`
- ES: `La composición funde la violencia sexual, la persecución divina y la justicia cósmica en una forma narrativa distintiva: crimen, tres plagas en escalada, juicio.`

- EN: `She is not wielding force against a defiant landscape. She is hunting a man who wronged her, and the entire land will suffer until she finds him.`
- ES: `No está aplicando la fuerza contra un paisaje rebelde. Está cazando a un hombre que la agravió, y todo el país sufrirá hasta que lo encuentre.`
- Note: «land» → «país» (sense: the whole polity, not soil).

## Failing gardener

- EN: `Shu-kale-tuda is a mortal gardener who tends a garden but struggles: his garden fails repeatedly and his crops wither.`
- ES: `Shu-kale-tuda es un jardinero mortal que cuida un huerto, pero se ve en apuros: su huerto fracasa una y otra vez y las cosechas se marchitan.`
- Note: «garden» → «huerto» (productive kitchen garden), not «jardín» (ornamental); crucial for «his crops wither».

- EN: `a gardener whose garden will not flourish is a man at the edge of existence.`
- ES: `un jardinero cuyo huerto no prospera es un hombre al borde de la existencia.`

## Shade-tree

- EN: `a tree -- possibly a poplar or Euphrates poplar -- that provides shade`
- ES: `un árbol —posiblemente un álamo o un álamo del Éufrates— que da sombra`
- Note: em-dash for asides, no inner spaces.

- EN: `For the first and last time in the narrative, Shu-kale-tuda does something right.`
- ES: `Por primera y última vez en el relato, Shu-kale-tuda hace algo bien.`

## Inanna first mention

- EN: `Inanna -- goddess of love, war, and the morning and evening star -- wearied from traversing heaven and earth, lies down to rest in the shade of the garden.`
- ES: `Inanna —diosa del amor, la guerra y el lucero del alba y del atardecer—, fatigada de recorrer el cielo y la tierra, se echa a descansar a la sombra del huerto.` [G: Inanna]
- Note: Appositive exists in the English source; glossary gloss aligns with it word-for-word, so no extra parenthetical needed.

## The violation

- ES: `Inanna no llora. No apela a la asamblea divina. No busca consejo de Enlil ni arbitraje de An.`

## First plague and fresh water

- EN: `where Enki, god of wisdom, fresh water, and cunning, lord of the _abzu_ beneath Eridu, ruled over the underground freshwater ocean`
- ES: `donde Enki, dios de la sabiduría, de las aguas dulces y de la astucia, señor del _abzu_ bajo Eridu, reinaba sobre el océano subterráneo de agua dulce` [G: Enki, abzu, Eridu]
- Notes: Gloss for Enki exists naturally in this appositive (matches glossary `first_mention_gloss_es`). «_abzu_» italicised per glossary; already glossed in earlier chapters, not re-glossed here.

- ES: `Inanna llena de sangre los pozos del País, o los contamina de algún otro modo.`
- Note: Capitalised «País» renders the source's capitalised «Land» (technical designation of Mesopotamia as a whole).

## Borderline decisions

1. **Inanna's appositive gloss**: the English source itself gives the appositive «goddess of love, war, and the morning and evening star». Since this matches the glossary `first_mention_gloss_es` exactly, I preserved the source appositive rather than inserting a second gloss.
2. **Enki's appositive in the first-plague paragraph**: the English has «where Enki, god of wisdom, fresh water, and cunning, lord of the _abzu_ beneath Eridu, ruled over the underground freshwater ocean». This is the text's own clause and already encompasses the glossary first-mention gloss for Enki. I preserved it literally and did not double-gloss when Enki reappears at line 76.
3. **«_me-lam_» first-mention gloss inserted** at line 63 in Spanish («ni _me-lam_ —el resplandor divino…—») because the term appears in this chapter for the first time in a disambiguating context; source has no parenthetical there. Consistent with chapters 08/11 handling.
4. **«Land» (capitalised) → «País» (capitalised)**: preserves the source's typographic emphasis designating Mesopotamia-as-polity; lower-case «país» elsewhere in narrative prose.
5. **«garden» → «huerto»** throughout: the text describes a productive plot with «crops», wells for irrigation, and a gardener struggling to make things grow — «huerto», not «jardín».
6. **«breaks badly» → «presenta roturas graves»**: material damage, not stylistic judgement.
7. **«single-prevalent-reconstructed» → «tradición única y predominante reconstruida»**: retained as a composite technical label matching chapter 11's «tradición única y predominante».
