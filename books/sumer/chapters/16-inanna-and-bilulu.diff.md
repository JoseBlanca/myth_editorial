## Opening paragraph

**Original:** The composition known to scholars as "Inanna and Bilulu" is catalogued as ETCSL 1.4.4 and comprises approximately 190 lines in the composite text.
**Edited:**  The composition scholars call "Inanna and Bilulu" is catalogued as ETCSL 1.4.4 and runs to roughly 190 lines in the composite text.
*Rationale:* "known to scholars as" → "scholars call" (kill nominalization / passive fat); "comprises approximately" → "runs to roughly" (tighter, less bureaucratic).

**Original:** Very few manuscript witnesses survive, indicating limited circulation compared to other Inanna and Dumuzid texts.
**Edited:**  Very few manuscript witnesses survive, which suggests limited circulation compared to other Inanna and Dumuzid texts.
*Rationale:* "indicating" is a dangling participle with no clear subject; "which suggests" tethers the inference to the preceding clause and hedges appropriately (scant manuscripts suggest, they do not prove).

**Original:** only a small number of fragmentary witnesses survive
**Edited:**  only a handful of fragmentary witnesses survive
*Rationale:* "a small number of" is a zombie-noun phrase for "a handful" / "a few."

**Original:** The present retelling follows the ETCSL composite text, supplemented by Jacobsen 1987, pp. 315–325.
**Edited:**  The present retelling follows that composite, supplemented by Jacobsen 1987, pp. 315–325.
*Rationale:* Avoids repeating "ETCSL composite text" within two sentences; "that composite" is a clean back-reference.

## Guilt-debate follow-up paragraph

**Original:** What we can say is that the composition places blame on these two figures, and that Inanna accepts this attribution of guilt as sufficient grounds for action.
**Edited:**  What we can say is that the composition blames these two figures, and that Inanna accepts the attribution as sufficient grounds for action.
*Rationale:* "places blame on" → "blames" (kill the nominalization); "this attribution of guilt" → "the attribution" (trims redundant "of guilt" — the antecedent makes it obvious).

**Original:** whether they represent something more diffuse
**Edited:**  whether they stand for something more diffuse
*Rationale:* "represent" is the heavier abstract verb; "stand for" is plainer and matches the mythic register.

**Original:** Jacobsen reads them as desert marauders, and his reading has the virtue of fitting the narrative logic: the shepherd dies in the steppe, and steppe-dwellers are held responsible.

*Rationale:* "has the virtue of fitting" is a zombie-noun construction — "fits" is the verb doing the work. "are held responsible" → "take the blame" gives an active echo of the preceding clause.

## Final two paragraphs

**Original:** Viewed within the Dumuzid cycle as a whole, the composition resolves the narrative arc that began with Inanna's descent to the underworld.
**Edited:**  Seen within the Dumuzid cycle as a whole, the composition resolves the narrative arc that began with Inanna's descent to the underworld.
*Rationale:* "Viewed" → "Seen": plainer Anglo-Saxon verb.

**Original:** grief is followed by the identification of those responsible, identification is followed by confrontation, and confrontation produces not merely punishment but the ritual framework through which the dead will be remembered.
**Edited:**  grief leads to identifying those responsible, identification leads to confrontation, and confrontation produces not merely punishment but the ritual framework through which the dead will be remembered.
*Rationale:* Two back-to-back passive "is followed by" constructions replaced with the active "leads to"; "the identification of those responsible" nominalization unpacked to "identifying those responsible." The third clause keeps "produces" to maintain the cumulative rhythm of the triad.

**Original:** The few scribes who copied this text — and it was a few, given the scant manuscript evidence — were preserving something more than a revenge tale.
**Edited:**  The few scribes who copied this text — and they were few, given the scant manuscript evidence — were preserving something more than a revenge tale.
*Rationale:* Grammatical agreement: "scribes" is plural, so "they were few" rather than the awkward "it was a few."

**Original:** his interpretation of the transformation is influential but should be read with awareness that it fills significant textual gaps interpretively.
**Edited:**  his reading of the transformation is influential but should be weighed with awareness that it fills significant textual gaps interpretively.
*Rationale:* Parallels the earlier "reading" variant introduced in the opening; "should be weighed" is more pointed than "should be read" (since the sentence is about the reader's critical stance, not literal reading).
