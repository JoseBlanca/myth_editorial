## 1. Headline findings

### 1.1 The catalog has a major omission
The single most consequential gap: **YHWH vs. Leviathan / Rahab** (Psalm 74, Isaiah 27:1, Job 26, Job 41). Gunkel's original 1895 *Chaoskampf* thesis was built on this corpus. The catalog currently folds it into "Revelation's Red Dragon," but the two are distinct — Rev 12 is the *New Testament eschatologisation* of the Hebrew Bible Leviathan/Rahab material (Yarbro Collins, Aune, Day, Bauckham). Treating them separately is non-negotiable.

### 1.2 Two core additions that scholars universally include
- **Ninurta vs. Asag (Lugal-e)** — the oldest full-length combat-cosmogony text; Sumerian, c. 2000 BCE. Wakeman, Forsyth, Annus.
- **Ninurta vs. Anzû** — the direct Mesopotamian ancestor of Marduk-Tiamat (Lambert, Annus).
- **Kumarbi Cycle / Song of Ullikummi (Hurro-Hittite)** — the Near-Eastern bridge to Greek Typhonomachy (West, López-Ruiz, Burkert).
- **Thraētaona vs. Aži Dahāka (Iranian)** — Calvert Watkins's flagship Indo-Iranian reflex.

### 1.3 Two current "core" entries are shakier than the draft admits
- **Apollo vs. Python** — Fontenrose himself framed this as cult-foundation, not cosmogony; the serpent represents a prior divine regime at Delphi. Medium confidence as core. Demote to Related is defensible.
- **Susanoo vs. Orochi** — Japanese-specialist scholarship (Isomae, Como, Ooms, Naumann) treats this as sword-etiology / metallurgical allegory / imperial-legitimation construction. The chaoskampf reading is a minority Western-comparativist position (Littleton 1981, Yoshida, Witzel). Medium confidence. Demote to Related is strongly indicated.

### 1.4 One current "related" entry should probably be promoted
- **Revelation 12 Red Dragon** — canonically secure, extensively treated in combat-myth scholarship (Yarbro Collins, Aune, Day, Bauckham). The 12:9 "ancient serpent" identification makes the Leviathan/Rahab lineage explicit. Promote to Core.

### 1.5 Multiple current "related" entries distort their traditions
Area specialists push back hard on chaoskampf framings of:
- **Nanabozho / Mishipeshu** (Anishinaabe) — Johnston, Smith, Vizenor and the Hallowellian "other-than-human persons" framework reject the chaos-vs-order mapping. The flood is reciprocity/vengeance, not chaos combat. **Recommend: demote to a contrastive case, or exclude.**
- **Väinämöinen / Iku-Turso** — Runo XLII is a shamanic *binding* (not slaying) in Lönnrot's 1849 editorial synthesis. Siikala, Honko, Kuusi resist the chaoskampf reading. **Recommend: exclude or demote to provisional.**
- **Mwindo / Kirimu** (Nyanga) — Biebuyck, Okpewho, Scheub, Belcher read the Kirimu-killing as a *hubris* episode for which Mwindo is punished by the sky-god. Moral valence is inverted from chaoskampf. **Recommend: exclude, or thin RELATED with the inversion made explicit.**

### 1.6 Three of four "provisional" entries are confirmed EXCLUDES
- **Mucalinda** — pure protection narrative, not combat. DeCaroli, Strong. **EXCLUDE (HIGH).**
- **Antaboga** — Śeṣa-type cosmogonic support, no combat episode. Hooykaas, Zoetmulder. **EXCLUDE (HIGH).**
- **Whowie** — single-source local dangerous-creature tale with colonial-appropriation problems. **EXCLUDE (HIGH).**
- **Rainbow Serpent** — Yingarna / Ngalyod / Wanambi / Wagyl / Yulunggul are distinct ancestral beings flattened by Radcliffe-Brown's 1926 pan-Aboriginal label; Merlan, Morphy, Keen, Rose have dismantled the lumping. **PROVISIONAL** only as a dedicated critique/methodological case; foregrounding the appropriation problem, not as a chaoskampf exemplar. Alternative: exclude.

### 1.7 Terminological debate you must flag in the book
Ballentine (*The Conflict Myth and the Biblical Tradition*, OUP 2015) argues "chaos" is the wrong word — the adversaries are better read as agents of an alternative divine power structure, not as chaos. The Eisenbrauns volume *Creation and Chaos* (Scurlock & Beal eds., 2013) is a direct reassessment of Gunkel. The book needs a prefatory note acknowledging this debate rather than adopting the 19th-century term uncritically.

## 3. DECIDE — cases that need your human call

The six cases below are the ones scholarship is genuinely split on, or where there is a real design choice. These are the only ones worth your reading time.

1. **Apollo vs. Python** — demote to RELATED, or keep CORE with caveat?
   - *Scholarship*: Fontenrose, Ogden → cult-foundation. Watkins cites as IE reflex briefly.
   - *Recommendation*: demote to RELATED. The demotion is substantively correct and you already flagged it as "lower-confidence core."
   - *Decision needed*: confirm the demotion.
   - Human decision: confirm the demotion.

2. **Susanoo vs. Orochi** — demote to RELATED, or keep CORE?
   - *Scholarship*: Japanese specialists (Naumann, Isomae, Como, Ooms) read it as sword/metallurgical/imperial-legitimation; chaoskampf reading is Littleton/Yoshida/Witzel comparativist minority.
   - *Recommendation*: demote to RELATED, with the chapter making explicit that the label is applied from outside.
   - *Decision needed*: confirm the demotion — this is a bigger change than Apollo/Python because Orochi is iconic.
   - Human decision: confirm the demotion.

3. **Vahagn vs. the Vishaps** — promote to CORE, or keep RELATED?
   - *Scholarship*: Russell, Petrosyan treat as securely attested IE-Armenian dragon-slayer via the epithet *Vishapakagh*, cult sites, vishapakar stones — but the narrative text is lost, only a hymn-paraphrase via Khorenatsi survives.
   - *Recommendation*: keep RELATED. The structural case is strong but the textual thinness is real.
   - *Decision needed*: confirm.
   - Human decision: keep related.

4. **Yu the Great / Xiangliu** — PROVISIONAL or RELATED?
   - *Scholarship*: Yu-flood core is hydraulic ordering (Lewis, Allan, Mathieu); only the peripheral Shanhaijing Xiangliu passage is a combat analogue.
   - *Recommendation*: PROVISIONAL. The draft's RELATED tier over-promises. Alternative: keep Yu in a separate non-combat-comparison sidebar and treat only Gonggong (new CORE) as the Chinese entry.
   - *Decision needed*: pick PROVISIONAL vs. exclude vs. sidebar.
   - Human decision: sidebar.

5. **Bida (Soninke)** — RELATED or PROVISIONAL?
   - *Scholarship*: the "reverse chaoskampf" reading is a modern comparativist overlay not established in Africanist literature (Monteil, Dieterlen, Conrad, Belcher read it as foundation-charter + climatological memory + genius-loci pact).
   - *Recommendation*: keep as RELATED *only* if you are writing the "inversion" chapter; otherwise demote to PROVISIONAL. The value of Bida for your book is exactly that it inverts the pattern — if you're not going to make that comparative move, it doesn't belong.
   - *Decision needed*: are you doing the inversion chapter?
   - Human decision: yes, do the inversion chapter.

6. **Rainbow Serpent** — PROVISIONAL critique-only, or exclude?
   - *Scholarship*: Merlan, Morphy, Keen, Rose have dismantled the pan-Australian lumping; no specialist reads these figures as chaos-combat.
   - *Recommendation*: PROVISIONAL only as a dedicated methodological section foregrounding the appropriation critique. Excluding it entirely is also defensible and possibly cleaner.
   - *Decision needed*: do you want the methodological chapter?
   - Human decision: do the methodological chapter.

Two more minor decisions if you want maximum rigor:

7. **Nanabozho / Mishipeshu** — exclude, or keep as dedicated contrastive case? Human decision: keep constrastive cate.
8. **Väinämöinen vs. Iku-Turso** — exclude, or keep as transformed-variant related? Human decision: keep as transformed-variant.
